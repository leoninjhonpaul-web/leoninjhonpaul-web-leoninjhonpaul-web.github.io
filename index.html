About Me

Hi! I’m Jhon Paul P. Leonin, 18-year-old IT student born on January 22, 2007 in Mamburao, Occidental Mindoro. I am currently studying at OMSC–San Jose Campus, where I continue to develop my skills in technology, web development, and digital creativity. I am passionate about building meaningful projects, exploring innovative ideas, and improving myself day by day.

I grew up in a supportive family. My mother, Esther P. Leonin, and my father, Francisco Y. Leonin Jr., have always inspired me to stay humble and determined. I have two brothers, Jaxith Pierre and Marc Justin Leonin, who motivate me to keep striving for success.

I enjoy doing various activities that help me relax and express myself. My hobbies include driving, singing, biking, traveling, taking photos, trying street foods, and exploring new environments. These activities allow me to learn, unwind, and appreciate the world around me.

As a person, I would describe myself as goal-oriented, disciplined, friendly, respectful, and consistently motivated. I always aim to grow not just as a student, but also as an individual. I admire people who work quietly, stay committed to their goals, and achieve success through perseverance and hard work.

After I graduate, I plan to pursue a career as a skilled programmer and also work toward my long-time dream of becoming a police officer. I believe that with dedication, continuous learning, and faith, I can achieve both paths.

Here are some of my personal favorites: I love listening to old songs, visiting beautiful places like the beach. My favorite colors are black and white for their simplicity and elegance, and my comfort food will always be French fries.

As I continue my journey in the IT field, my goal is to create projects that are not only functional but also inspiring. I want to contribute to solutions that make life easier, encourage creativity, and reflect my passion for technology and innovation.
